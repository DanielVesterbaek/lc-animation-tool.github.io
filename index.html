Vehicle Gallery

Get started quickly by picking one of the vehicles from the Gallery. You can easily add more vehicles to the Gallery thanks to the .lcl files.

Local Vehicle

Use the Quick Tag Tool, to tag the Body and Wheels of the vehicle and easily rig any model. For CAD datasets, use the CAD Data Setup tool.

Append/Link Vehicle

You can append rigged LC vehicles from another Blender Scene Files. With Launch Control Pro, you can also Link Vehicles and use Library Overrides.

Camber/Caster

The Rig comes with realistic camber and caster settings, ensuring Authentic Performance out of the box. Easily Customize these parameters in the UI.

Automatic Suspension

The rig features built-in Suspension logic, giving you full control. Adjust Suspension Travel and Bottom Out Height effortlessly to match your model.

Steering System

Launch Control leverages real-world Ackermann Steering Geometry. For added flexibility, custom handles let you Art-Direct steering with accuracy.

Speed Segments

Easily animate your vehicle with Speed Keyframes directly in the viewport. Launch Control handles the Interpolation, giving you smooth results.

Animation Presets

Jumpstart your workflow with the library of built-in Presets, including Slaloms, Loops and more. You can also Save Custom Presets to streamline projects.

Workflow Preferences

As with all features in Launch Control, the Speed Segment tool is Fully Customizable—offering an intuitive setup while also allowing Fine-Tuning.

Real-Time Playback

Calculate realistic physics Live in the viewport. No need to wait for baking or caching when setting up your scene.

G-Force Indicator

Learn from the physics! Check the actual G-Forces acting on the vehicle during corners and accelerations. Use this to improve the Realism of your animations.

Physics Overdrive

Use the Artist-Friendly Overdrive sliders to Art-Direct the result of the physics with ease. Adjust with no re-baking to efficiently get the right feeling across.

Headlight Beams

Easily set up headlight beams for night time animations. Select one of 6 different beam types and customize spread, sharpness and temperature.

Automatic Skidmarks

Generate Skidmarks from the vehicle as it’s driving. The material of the Skidmarks can be customized for an altered look.

DCC Bridge

Pipe the animation into other DCCs such as UE5, Cinema 4D, Houdini and more using the DCC Bridge. Supported formats: .abc, .fbx, .usd, .glb, .udatasmith

Proxy Tool

Work more efficiently with dense vehicle data thanks to the one-click Proxy Tool. Export the Proxy Model and reconnect the High Quality data for ultimate speed.

Extras In the box!

Export to DCCs

Quick Export makes data transfer fast and painless. LC plays well with UE 5, Omniverse, C4D, and other DCCs and gives you useful export options to fit your workflow.

Skidmark Generator

One-click setup and results right away.
Use the intensity and variance sliders to adjust the look of the skidmarks.

Automatic Camber & Castor

Wheels do not just turn around the Z-axis like most of us imagine. Camber and Caster are important to make the wheel motion look right.

Ackermann Steering Geometry

Car animation always look great – until the car is going through a curve. Ackermann in LC takes care of that and lets you put your focus on what matters.

3 HQ Vehicle Models Included

LC comes with 3 high quality vehicle models. All shaded and ready to be rigged. Take advantage of the Vehicle Gallery to quickly drop these into your projects.

Multi-Car Workflow

Rig and animate as many vehicles as you want. Color-coded driving paths and the Multi-Edit mode makes it easy to control each individual vehicle or all vehicles at once.

Speedometer

Knowing the speed at any moment in time is crucial for realism. Read the speed from inside the 3D View or in the UI panel.

Automated Ground Detection

Place all objects the vehicle is interacting with into the “Ground Detection” collection. High poly, low poly, messy geo, it doesn’t matter.

HDR Headlight Texture Presets

6 included headlight presets automatically sets up nice light beams in front of the car. The beams play well with volume objects, to create a sense of depth in the scene.
